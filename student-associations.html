学生社团Student Associations

以下为目前与《周易》学习与青年研究相关的两个学生组织。The following are two student organizations currently associated with I Ching learning and youth scholarship.

PKU

北京大学易学社Peking University I Ching Society

北京大学易学社成立于 2005 年，为北京大学团委正式审批的学生社团组织。该社团是以易经为中心展开学习、研讨活动的学术类社团。经过二十余年的发展，社团已经成为北大校内颇有影响的知名社团。

Peking University I-Ching Society (PKUICS), established in 2005, is an official student organization approved by the Communist Youth League Committee of Peking University. As an academic society, its core mission revolves around the systematic study and scholarly discussion of the I Ching (The Book of Changes). After more than two decades of development, the society has grown into a highly influential and well-known student organization within the university.

SHOU

上海海洋大学周易学社Shanghai Ocean University Zhouyi Society

上海海洋大学周易学社成立于 2025 年，为上海海洋大学团委正式批准的学生社团组织。该社团以学术研究为基石，理性探索易学文化的历史脉络与哲学内涵，推动传统智慧在现代生活中的传承和应用，培养学生跨学科思维能力，在哲学、法学、文学、中医学之间建立理解结构，提升学生逻辑思维与系统思考能力。

Shanghai Ocean University Zhouyi Society, established in 2025, is an official student organization formally approved by the Communist Youth League Committee of Shanghai Ocean University. Grounded in academic research, the society aims to rationally explore the historical evolution and philosophical connotations of I Ching culture. It seeks to promote the inheritance and practical application of traditional wisdom in modern life while cultivating students' interdisciplinary thinking. By establishing conceptual frameworks across the fields of philosophy, law, literature, and Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), the society focuses on enhancing students' logical reasoning and systems thinking capabilities.

学生社团的学术意义The Academic Role of Student Associations

学生社团不仅是校园文化组织，也可以成为经典学习、问题讨论与青年学术训练的重要入口。围绕《周易》形成的学生共同体，有助于在本科与研究生阶段培养系统思维、跨学科理解与持续性讨论能力。

Student associations are not merely campus cultural organizations. They can also serve as important entry points into classical learning, problem-oriented inquiry, and junior scholarly training. Student communities organized around the I Ching help cultivate systems thinking, interdisciplinary understanding, and sustained habits of discussion at both undergraduate and graduate levels.
